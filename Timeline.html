Civil War Reconstruction Timeline

1622

First African slaves are brought to British owned colonies in the Americas

February 28, 1854

The Republican party is formed

November 6, 1860

Abraham Lincoln is elected president and South Carolina succeeds.

February 4, 1861

Seven states that have succeeded form a new union called the confederacy

January 1, 1863

Abraham Lincoln declares the emancipation proclamation freeing all slaves in confederate states

April 9, 1865

1865 Robert E. Lee surrenders

April 14, 1865

Abraham Lincoln is shot and killed

May 29, 1865

President Andrew Johnson announces his plan for the Reconstruction of the South which happens to be so lenient it returns many confederates to power

Summer, 1865

Southern states create laws coined black codes to make life more difficult for black Americans.

December, 1865

Politician Carl Schurz reports on black southerners needing government protection from hostile white southerners. While the report is ignored by Andrew Johnson, northerners are horrified.

January, 1866

Alexander Stephens former VP of the confederacy is elected to the Senate initiated by Andrew Johnson however congress rejects the plan.

April 9, 1866

Radical republicans overrule a veto for the Civil Rights Bill. This bill makes all persons born in the U.S (with the exception of native americans) will be granted citizenship.

June 13, 1866

Congress approves the 14th amendment which prevents any state from denying equality to any male citizen.

July 16, 1866

Congress overrules Johnson's veto to extend the life and expand the powers of the Freedmen's Bureau.

November 1866

Radical Republicans gain control of every Northern state legislature and government in the midterms

March 2, 1867

Overruling Andrew Johnson's veto, Congress passes the first in a series of Reconstruction Acts. This divided the South into five military districts, meant to be run by military leaders till the states met the federal requirements for forming new governments. In order to prevent Johnson from overriding the Republicans' Reconstruction efforts, Congress passed the Tenure of Office Act. This prevents the president from dismissing government officials who have been approved by Congress.

May, 1867

Nathan Bedford Forrest, former confederate general becomes the first grand wizard of newly formed organization called the K.K.K (Ku Klux Klan)

Fall, 1867

Former confederate states hold constitutional conventions. Roughly one and a half million voters are registered, seven hundred thousand being African American.

February, 1868

Following the dismissal of war secretary Edwin Stanton congress impeaches Andrew Johnson for having violated the Tenure Office Act among other charges.

May 16, 1868

Andrew Johnson is acquitted from having violated the Tenure Office Act, and survives dismissal from office by one vote.

June, 1868

Alabama, Arkansas, Florida, Georgia, Louisiana, North Carolina, and South Carolina, are allowed back into the union as part of the reconstruction plan.

November 3, 1868

Ulysses S. Grant is elected president

February 26, 1869

Former President of the confederacy Jefferson Davis, is released from prison

May, 1869

The National Women's Suffrage Association (NWSA) is formed

February 25, 1870

Hiram Revels of Mississippi, becomes the first black senator by taking former confederate president Jefferson Davis’ seat.

March 30, 1870

The fifteenth amendment became part of the constitution. The fifteenth amendment prevents states from denying people the right to vote based on their race, color, or prior condition of servitude
